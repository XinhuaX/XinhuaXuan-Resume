As a youth immigrant, I settled down in a city with a few foreign-born populations since my parents wanted me to learn mainstream American culture. However, being culturally misunderstood and sidelined in my middle school broke my expectation of unbiasedly getting an education in a society that promotes equality. After a-semester-long struggling, I, fortunately, received assistance from a small local organization in assimilating into the community. It dawned on me that bridging cultural gaps is the key to eliminating discrimination.

I felt a strong responsibility to stand with the local organization as a united alliance to disseminate immigrant culture to my city. Advised by organization staff, I volunteered as a teacher assistant at Golden Key Chinese School after I successfully transferred to the Honor English class from the ESL class. I wanted to utilize my bilingual skill to spread Chinese immigrant culture to the locals. I first worked with seven native students. Noticing they had little motivation to learn Chinese from their late-due homework, I talked with them individually. They thought learning Chinese is dispensable because of the few Chinese populations in the city. Instead of boring textbooks, I promoted a more interactive teaching method to my supervisor to arouse students' interests.

I transmitted the class mode by holding a dumpling party since my students love food. I taught them how to make dumplings in the class, and they frequently asked questions about the ingredients. Their positive reaction gave me confidence, so I improved their listening and pronunciation by Chinese pop music, historical drama, and variety shows. By the end of that semester, my students all passed the Chinese level test for the first time. Seeing my success, my supervisor gradually transmitted all classes by using the new teaching method, and the number of our students had doubled in the second year of my volunteering. More students embraced Chinese culture and behaved friendly to their immigrant classmates. It reinforced my belief that eliminating cultural gaps can reduce discrimination and promote harmony in our community. I will always stand on the frontline in the fight against cultural gaps.
